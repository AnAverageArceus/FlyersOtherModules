On the Subject of Singularity Wires

It's just two wires, right? Why do we bring up more of those anyway?

This module consists of two wires connected to a round screen at the center. The screen may or may not show anything at a certain time interval. To disarm this module, you must disarm ALL instances of Singularity Wires attached to the given casing by interacting with them in a specific way. An incorrect combination of actions on the wires will cause a strike. The display on Singularity Wires may or may not change after a strike. The color of the wires will not change, however.

Appendix: The Disarm Button

It's there for a reason. And yes, you may not like it at all.

On the back of the module is a red rectangular button that says "DISARM MODULE MANUALLY." When pressed, this will automatically disarm the given Singularity Wires only when Singularity Wires are put in a "solve state". Due to strange natures on the "solve detection system" for modules that handle synchronized solves like Singularity Wires, all Singularity Wires will flip to the back due to the fact that there is no way Singularity Wires could directly connect to a module that relies on solves without an override. Notable actions that forbid synchronized solves are, reading a stage on major modules that require an input at the end, and inputting an answer on a module before another solve. No time, solve or strike dependent actions are required for the disarm button to be pressed.

Originally, Singularity Wires was going to detect if certain modules were present on the bomb or if there was 1 instance of Singularity Wires. In summary, the effort to set up a system for solve dependent detection system was not worth the time at the end.
